Gastric Sleeve Surgery

Gastric sleeve surgery (sleeve gastrectomy) removes roughly 75–80% of the stomach, leaving a narrow, sleeve-shaped stomach. This limits how much food you can eat at one time and reduces the hormones that drive appetite — helping patients achieve significant, lasting weight loss.

The procedure is performed laparoscopically, meaning smaller incisions, less pain, and a faster return to normal activity compared to open surgery.

Procedure time60–90 minutes
Hospital stay1–2 nights
Back to light activity1–2 weeks
AnesthesiaGeneral anesthesia

Who is it for?

Gastric sleeve surgery is generally considered for patients with a BMI of 35+ (or 30+ with obesity-related conditions) who haven't achieved lasting results through diet and lifestyle changes alone.

What to expect

  • A full pre-operative assessment to confirm the procedure is right for you
  • Laparoscopic surgery under general anesthesia
  • 1–2 nights of hospital monitoring after the procedure
  • A structured nutrition plan to support your recovery and results
  • Follow-up visits to track your progress over time
